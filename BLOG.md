# Building Cadence: From Missed Deadlines to an AI Academic Assistant

## The Problem We Lived Every Semester

Every university student has experienced it.

You open Moodle and find an assignment due tomorrow.

A quiz announcement was posted in a WhatsApp group.

A project deadline was shared on Google Classroom.

A club event is sitting in your Google Calendar.

Somewhere in between all of this, an email contains another submission date that you completely forgot about.

The information exists, but it is scattered across multiple platforms.

The problem is not that students don't care about deadlines.

The problem is that deadlines are everywhere.

As students ourselves, we faced this challenge repeatedly. We missed assignments, started projects late, and realized only a few days before submission that multiple deadlines had collided in the same week.

We noticed that our classmates were facing exactly the same issue.

That made us ask a simple question:

**What if a system could warn us before our schedule became overwhelming?**

That question became the foundation of Cadence.

---

# Why Existing Planners Aren't Enough

There are countless calendar applications and task managers available today.

They are excellent at recording deadlines.

But they expect students to manually interpret everything.

A calendar can tell you that you have five submissions next week.

It cannot tell you that next Wednesday will require twelve hours of work.

It cannot explain which deadlines are creating the workload spike.

It cannot suggest what you should start working on today.

In short, existing tools are reactive.

We wanted to build something proactive.

---

# The Birth of Cadence

During the AI Buildathon, our team had only seven days to transform an idea into a working product.

From the beginning, we knew we didn't want to create another planner.

We wanted to create an intelligent academic assistant.

An assistant that could analyze deadlines, estimate workload, detect conflicts, and recommend a balanced study plan before students reached a point of stress.

That vision became Cadence.

---

# What Cadence Does

Cadence takes academic tasks such as assignments, quizzes, projects, and examinations and analyzes them collectively.

Instead of showing isolated deadlines, it predicts workload intensity across the semester.

If multiple important submissions are approaching simultaneously, Cadence identifies the collision and warns the student early.

Based on task priority, difficulty, estimated effort, and available study hours, it generates a personalized study schedule that distributes work across multiple days.

Rather than encouraging last-minute cramming, Cadence promotes consistent progress.

---

# Building in Seven Days

Building a complete academic planning platform within a week was challenging.

Every day brought new ideas, new problems, and new iterations.

The biggest technical challenge was integrating the frontend and backend while designing a workload prediction engine that produced meaningful recommendations instead of arbitrary schedules.

Creating an intuitive user interface was equally important.

Students interact with planners every day, so the experience needed to be simple, clean, and informative.

Balancing feature development, debugging, deployment, and UI refinement within seven days pushed us to make careful engineering decisions and prioritize what truly mattered.

Despite the time constraints, seeing the final product come together was one of the most rewarding experiences of the buildathon.

---

# AI with Purpose

Artificial Intelligence is often added to projects simply because it is trending.

We wanted AI to solve a real problem.

Cadence uses intelligent planning and workload analysis to help students make better decisions.

Instead of replacing students, it augments their planning process by providing recommendations that are explainable and actionable.

Our long-term vision includes personalized effort estimation, adaptive scheduling, natural language study recommendations, and AI-powered productivity coaching.

The goal is not to automate education.

The goal is to help students learn with less stress and greater consistency.

# Challenges Beyond Code

The biggest challenge was not writing code.

It was deciding what to build.

With only seven days available, every new feature meant sacrificing time elsewhere.

We constantly asked ourselves:

Will this feature genuinely help students?

Does it solve the original problem?

Can we deliver it with quality?

These questions helped shape Cadence into a focused solution instead of an overloaded application.
